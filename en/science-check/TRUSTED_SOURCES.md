# Reliable sources — Consultation priority

## Tier 1 — Gold standard (prioritize)

| Source | Type | Access |
|---|---|---|
| Cochrane Library | Systematic reviews | cochranelibrary.com |
| PubMed / PMC | Meta-analyses, RCTs | pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov |
| Examine.com | Evidence-based summaries with grades | examine.com |

## Tier 2 — Institutional sources

| Source | Type | Access |
|---|---|---|
| EFSA | Scientific opinions EU | efsa.europa.eu |
| FDA | US regulatory positions | fda.gov |
| ANSES | FR health safety opinions | anses.fr |
| WHO / WHO | Global recommendations | who.int |
| EMA | European medicines agency | ema.europa.eu |

## Tier 3 — Reliable popularization

| Source | Type | Access |
|---|---|---|
| Harvard Health | Medical popularization | health.harvard.edu |
| Mayo Clinic | Health fact sheets | mayoclinic.org |
| McGill OSS | Critical analysis | mcgill.ca/oss |
| NHS | UK health fact sheets | nhs.uk |
| Science-Based Medicine | Critical blog | sciencebasedmedicine.org |

## Tier 4 — Verification and reporting

| Source | Usage |
|---|---|
| Retraction Watch | Check if a study was retracted |
| Semantic Scholar | Citation count, impact |
| Google Scholar | Cross-cited sources |

## Sources to avoid or flag

- Blogs without peer review
- Commercial sites selling the product studied
- Studies funded exclusively by the industry concerned (flag the conflict)
- Unreplicated preprints (bioRxiv, medRxiv) — usable as a signal but not as proof
