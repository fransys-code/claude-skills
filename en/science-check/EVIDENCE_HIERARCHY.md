# Evidence levels grid

## Hierarchy (strongest to weakest)

| Level | Study type | Weight | Use in verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1a | Meta-analysis of RCTs / Cochrane Review | Very strong | Can justify "CONFIRMED" or "REFUTED" alone |
| 1b | Large RCT (n>200) double blind | Strong | Confirmed if replicated by a 2nd RCT |
| 2 | Small RCT (n<200) or single blind | Moderate | "PARTIALLY CONFIRMED" if coherent |
| 3 | Cohort / observational study | Weak | Correlation, not causation — flag |
| 4 | Animal study | Very weak | "PREMATURE" — not directly transposable |
| 5 | In vitro (lab cells) | Minimal | "PREMATURE" — plausible mechanism only |
| 6 | Expert opinion / tradition / testimonials | Null | "NOT PROVEN" — does not constitute proof |

## Overall verdict assignment rules

| Situation | Verdict |
|---|---|
| Positive meta-analysis + no major contradiction | CONFIRMED |
| Multiple positive RCTs but no meta-analysis | PARTIALLY CONFIRMED |
| Contradictory results between comparable quality studies | PARTIALLY CONFIRMED (flag the controversy) |
| Only animal / in vitro studies | PREMATURE |
| No quality studies found | NOT PROVEN |
| Negative meta-analysis or majority of negative RCTs | REFUTED |

## Biases to flag systematically

- **Industry funding**: studies funded by the product manufacturer
- **Small sample**: n < 50 in an RCT
- **Short duration**: study < 4 weeks for a long-term effect
- **Non-representative population**: only young men, only athletes, etc.
- **Surrogate endpoints**: biological marker instead of real clinical outcome
- **Publication bias**: only positive results published in the field
