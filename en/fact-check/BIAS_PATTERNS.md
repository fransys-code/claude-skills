# Bias and Manipulation Patterns to Detect

## Presentation Bias

| Pattern | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Clickbait / sensationalism | Exaggerated headline vs. content | "Jackpot! France discovers $92 billion treasure" |
| Unjustified superlatives | "greatest", "unique in the world", "historic" without comparison | "The world's largest reserve" without noting it's an estimate |
| Impressive figures without context | Large number without scale of comparison | "46 million tons" without saying it's theoretical |
| False exclusive | "revelation", "we're hiding this" when info is public | "What the media won't tell you..." |

## Reasoning Bias

| Pattern | Description | How to Detect |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Correlation = causation | Two correlated events presented as cause-effect link | Check if an interventional study exists |
| Cherry-picking | Selecting data supporting the thesis, omitting the rest | Compare with complete data |
| Overgeneralization | One particular case extrapolated to the whole | Verify sample size and representativeness |
| Appeal to authority | "Scientists say..." without naming or citing | Verify who said what exactly |
| Straw man | Distorting the opposing position to refute it | Compare with the actual position |
| Slippery slope | "If X then inevitably Y then catastrophic Z" | Verify each step of reasoning |

## Omission Bias

| Pattern | Description | How to Detect |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Omission of context | True fact but deprived of context | Search for complete context |
| Omission of uncertainty | Estimate presented as certainty | Search for words "estimate", "could", "if confirmed" in primary source |
| Omission of risks | Benefits without mentioning drawbacks | Search for "[subject] risks limitations criticisms" |
| Temporal omission | No date, or date hidden for old information | Verify publication date and date of facts |

## Source Bias

| Pattern | Description | How to Detect |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Conflict of interest | Author or funder has stake in conclusion | Search for who funds, who sells |
| Echo chamber | Multiple articles citing each other without primary source | Trace back the citation chain |
| Disguised press release | News article that's actually a company press release | Compare with original press release |
| Industry-funded study | Research paid by a company benefiting from results | Check "funding" section of papers |

## Quick Detection Checklist

Before concluding, verify:
- [ ] Does the headline match the article content?
- [ ] Do cited figures have an identifiable source?
- [ ] Are citations in context?
- [ ] Are cited experts in the right domain?
- [ ] Are limitations and uncertainties mentioned?
- [ ] Is there a potential conflict of interest?
- [ ] Is the information picked up by Tier 1-2 sources?
